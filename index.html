Temperature measuring system using wheatstone bridge:

The Wheatstone bridge is a circuit that consists of four resistors connected in a diamond configuration. It is used to measure electrical resistance accurately, and it has various applications, including temperature sensing, pressure sensing, and strain gauges. In temperature sensing applications, a sensor whose resistance changes with temperature is used as one of the bridge resistors. As the temperature changes, the resistance of the sensor changes, causing the bridge to become unbalanced. The voltage across the load is measured and used to calculate the resistance of the sensor, which can be used to determine the temperature. To build a temperature measuring system using a Wheatstone bridge, a temperature sensor is chosen and incorporated into the circuit along with three other resistors to form a balanced bridge. The voltage across the bridge is measured and used to calculate the resistance of the sensor, which can be used to determine the temperature. The system can display the temperature reading on a screen or transmit it wirelessly to another device. This approach offers high accuracy and stability, making it ideal for applications where precision is crucial.

Building Difference Amplifier Circuit:

A difference amplifier, also known as differential amplifier, is an electronic circuit that amplifies the voltage difference between two input signals while rejecting any signals that are common to both inputs. It is commonly used in signal processing applications to measure the difference between two signals, such as in audio applications to remove common-mode noise or in instrumentation to amplify small differential signals for measurement purposes. The output of a difference amplifier is proportional to the voltage difference between the two input signals, multiplied by the gain of the amplifier. The gain can be adjusted by changing the values of the resistors in the circuit.


Claculating gain of difference amplifier:


$$V_{out}=\frac{R_2}{R_1}\times(V_b-V_a)$$

$$2=\frac{R_2}{R_1}\times(2.5-2.7)$$

$$\frac{R_2}{R_1}=\frac{2}{(2.7-2.5)}$$

$$\frac{R_2}{R_1}=10$$

∴ Gain= 10

Circuit Flaws:

The ciruit built as seen in figure was affected by loading. The loading effect occurs because the output impedance of the amplifier interacts with the load impedance, resulting in a voltage drop across the output impedance of the amplifier. This voltage drop can reduce the output voltage, increase distortion, and limit the amount of power that the amplifier can deliver to the load.To solve this we connected the outputs of the wheastone bridge to buffers.A buffer circuit has a high input impedance and a low output impedance, which helps to minimize the loading effect. The high input impedance ensures that the buffer circuit does not draw any current from the previous stage, while the low output impedance ensures that the buffer circuit can drive the next stage with a low voltage drop. This ensures that the signal is not attenuated or distorted as it passes from one stage to the next.
